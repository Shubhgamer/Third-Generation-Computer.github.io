Third Generation Computer (1964-1971)

The third generation computer used intehrated circuits (ICs) in which the small transistors were placed on silicon chips; called semiconductorss. IBM 360; ICL 1900 and VAX-750 are example of third generation computer.

Advantages
These computers were smaller in size than the computers of earlier generations.
Thier speed and accuracy were better than the first and second generation computers.
They generated lesser amount of heat and were less expensive than the computers of earlier generations.
They were based on high-level language which was easier to understand and machine-independent.
Disadvantages
They required air conditioning.
Highly sophisticated technology was required for the manufacturing of IC chips.
